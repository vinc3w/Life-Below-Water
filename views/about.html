Overview

Ocean pollution in Malaysia is a multifaceted problem with far-reaching consequences. It adversely affects marine life by disrupting ecosystems, harming iconic species such as sea turtles and dolphins, and degrading coral reefs. Moreover, the pollution jeopardizes the well-being of coastal communities, which rely on healthy oceans for their sustenance. To address these issues, Malaysia has implemented environmental regulations and monitoring mechanisms, though the challenges persist.The website provides a wealth of background information, including statistics, data, and real-life stories to illustrate the extent and impact of ocean pollution. Additionally, the site emphasizes the urgent need for action, encouraging visitors to get involved in various ways, from supporting local initiatives to advocating for policy changes. Ultimately, it seeks to raise awareness, inspire action, and foster a greater sense of responsibility in safeguarding Malaysia's precious marine ecosystems.

Objectives
  1. Awareness: Encouraging awareness through compelling visuals and impactful stories.
  2. Advocacy: Providing information on advocacy efforts, campaigns, and how visitors can get involved.
  3. Conservation: Highlighting conservation initiatives and organizations working to mitigate pollution.
Why it matters?

Ocean pollution can lead to many problems to marine ecosystem and so is human's. If not taken care, it could lead to serious damage to the marine life and cause health risks to humans. Therefore, it is our responsibillity to ensure the cleanliness of the ocean.
